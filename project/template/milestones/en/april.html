STIMULATING INNOVATION AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP

 

Every year, iMinds organises the iBoot coaching program for entrepreneurial minds who have a specific ICT business idea. Teams are assembled during this program, and each of them draws up a highly evolved business plan over the course of intensive coaching sessions. The grand finale arrives when teams present their business plan to a panel composed of entrepreneurs and investors.

In April 2012, seven companies were selected to participate in the intensive and international training and coaching, which took them to the different aspects of creating a business plan. Learn about the ideas:

Read&Play

Idea: an app for tablet and browser presenting 21st century fables to 21st century kids who prefer gaming above reading.

Owner: Ianka Fleerackers

 

Caphe

Idea: software to facilitate the speedier and more accurate design of optical chips.

Owner: Martin Fiers

 

Axsmate

Idea: making use of mobile phone and cloud based services for remote access use cases

Owner: Mick de Valck

 

Graphine

Idea: a middleware solution for (game)developers, that helps to create and visualise gigapixel texture datasets.

Owner: Charles Hollemeersch

 

Dyamand

Idea: an easily adjustable application framework for remote control of networked devices and services.

Owner: Jelle Nelis

 

Lionfish

Idea: a complementary team working on innovation for events

Owner: Thomas Deckers

 

Cloud Friends

Idea: bringing the digital home to the cloud.

Owner: Erik Horemans

 

Under the guidance of national and international coaches, the teams spent five months drawing up a business plan, with 4 of those companies advancing to the final panel round – Graphine, Axsmate, Cloud Friends and Caphe.

Graphine was unanimously voted the winner of this edition thanks to its unique technology, solid value proposition, complementary team and persuasive pitch. This means that the team went straight to the final selection round of the iStart incubation program.

“It is crucial that we can support entrepreneurs who have a worthwhile business idea through programs such as iBoot and iStart and in so doing maximize their chances of building a successful business. After all, innovation goes hand in hand with social and economic impact.”
