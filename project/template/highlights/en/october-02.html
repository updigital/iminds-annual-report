PARTNERSHIPS

iMinds strengthens partnerships with universities

In 2012, iMinds renewed its cooperation agreements with the five Flemish universities: Ghent University, Free University of Brussels, University of Antwerp, Hasselt University and Catholic University of Leuven. The renewed agreements ensure an increased uniformity in the cooperation with universities. This makes it easier to initiate research projects and creates a clear framework for all of the partners involved.

RESEARCH

Active in advanced research

The iMinds research departments are at the forefront of the national and international research community. As an example, the Future Media & Imaging research department is a key partner in the SIMRET project. This program is currently developing a new medical imaging technique for a hybrid scanner that can make SPECT and MR images simultaneously, leading to more accurate diagnoses of patients. This is just one of the many examples of how iMinds researchers contribute to a better world for everyone.

KIC ICT LABS

Supporting European entrepreneurship in digital innovation

In late 2012, iMinds became Associate Partner of the Knowledge and Innovation Community (KIC) ICT Labs of the European Institute for Innovation and Technology (EIT). EIT ICT Labs combines education, research and industry in a European ecosystem of entrepreneurs. Their aim is to turn Europe into a world leader in ICT innovation. The membership offers iMinds many opportunities to strengthen its international presence.

LIVING LABS

iMinds coordinates European Network of Living Labs

In 2012, iMinds was re-elected as European secretary of the European Network of Living Labs (ENoLL), the international federation of recognized Living Labs in Europe and the rest of the world. A living lab is a real life test and experimentation environment where users and producers create innovations together. ENoLL today unites more than 300 labs worldwide, of which 30 are active at the European level.
